grandpa poem

What is there to fear, When our things are broken? 
The things we hold dear, At times, harsh words spoken. 
We fuss and we fret, We complain to our friends, 

But do we forget, To find sweet amends?
In fact, things must break, Before they can mend. 
It’s a process we take, Until journey’s end. 

Again, ground is broken, Again, plant more seeds. 
Nourish and care, A new plant, in the weeds. 
So the next time I find my things, Broken in half
I should be more grateful, Maybe chuckle, or laugh.


Ground must be broken, To receive a new seed. 
The seed must then sprout, For such is the need. 
The seed must be buried, In dirt and manure, 

Scraped, poked and prodded, Perhaps with a skewer. 
In time things will grow, As the soil is broken, 
It does so in silence, With not a word spoken. 

Again, ground is broken, Again, plant more seeds. 
Nourish and care, A new plant, in the weeds. 
So the next time I find my things, Broken in half
I should be more grateful, Maybe chuckle, or laugh.


A plant starts to form, Its shape starts to show. 
From soil twice broken, That little plant grows. 
But soon it’s surrounded, By things we call weeds,
 
The farmer confounded, Trying to meet that plant’s needs. 
They grow up together, This plant and the weeds. 
He waters and nourishes, All that plant’s needs. 

Again, ground is broken, Again, plant more seeds. 
Nourish and care, A new plant, in the weeds. 
So the next time I find my things, Broken in half
I should be more grateful, Maybe chuckle, or laugh.
